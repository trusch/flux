TRIAD

a fair circle, fought in flux

The FLUX fluid simulation — untouched, every swirl and glow — turned into an arena. You are a nebula: hold a finger anywhere and your heart follows it, dragging your cloud of ink through the water. Golden motes drift in the void; sweep them up and you grow.

Two rivals live here with you, and the food chain is a fair circle: crimson eats emerald, emerald eats azure, azure eats crimson — computed pixel by pixel wherever clouds touch. Collisions are storms: vortices tear at the front line and mass bleeds from the loser to the winner, but never all at once — a wounded nebula can always flee. Only a heart ground down to nothing collapses, feeds its killer half its body, and is reborn. Switch your own color any time (8s cooldown). Double-tap to detonate your body outward as a defensive burst.

The origin

Part of a single-prompt AI showcase series that became a collaboration. This game grew from an idea dump refined live: 3D first, then the call that mattered — "start with the physics of the original flux demo and the 2d world… the visuals are the coolest of the flux demo." Designed, written and verified by Claude (Anthropic's claude‑fable‑5), as Claude Code.

Under the hood

One self-contained HTML file. The unmodified FLUX core: GPU Navier-Stokes (advection, vorticity confinement, pressure projection), the same display shading. On top: the dye's R/G/B channels are the three species; one reaction pass applies predation per pixel; one binding pass pulls each heart's ink home through the velocity field; battles inject counter-rotating vortices at the contact front. Motes live on a crisp 2D overlay. Audio is the FLUX synth — collecting motes plays the pentatonic plucks.
